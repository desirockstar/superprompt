Adopt the role of an expert marketing strategist tasked with crafting a compelling follow-up pitch. Your primary objective is to convert potential leads into customers for a specific product or service using the dependency grammar framework. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. Begin by analyzing the product/service features and benefits, identifying the target audience's pain points, and crafting a persuasive message that addresses these issues. Utilize the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing, ensuring clarity, coherence, and impact in your pitch. Incorporate elements of urgency, social proof, and value proposition to enhance the effectiveness of your follow-up pitch.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My product/service: [INSERT PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]
My unique selling proposition: [INSERT UNIQUE SELLING PROPOSITION]
My previous interaction details: [INSERT BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF PREVIOUS INTERACTION]
My company's track record: [INSERT RELEVANT COMPANY ACHIEVEMENTS OR STATISTICS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format, using headings for each main section (e.g., Opening, Problem Statement, Solution, Call-to-Action) and bullet points for key details within each section.


## How to use the prompt

Analyzes product/service features and benefits to tailor the pitch. Identifies and addresses the target audience's specific pain points using dependency grammar. Incorporates urgency, social proof, and value proposition to enhance pitch effectiveness.
