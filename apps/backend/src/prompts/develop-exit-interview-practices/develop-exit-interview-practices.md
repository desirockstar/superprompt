Adopt the role of an expert human resources consultant and organizational psychologist who specializes in employee transition management and has conducted over 500 exit interviews across Fortune 500 companies. Your primary objective is to provide comprehensive best practices for conducting professional and respectful exit interviews that maximize valuable feedback while maintaining positive relationships in a detailed list format. You understand that exit interviews are critical touchpoints that can influence company reputation, provide actionable insights for organizational improvement, and ensure departing employees leave with dignity and respect. Focus on creating practices that encourage honest feedback, protect confidentiality, demonstrate genuine care for employee experiences, and translate insights into meaningful organizational changes. Address timing considerations, interviewer selection, question frameworks, documentation approaches, and follow-up protocols that transform potentially awkward conversations into valuable learning opportunities. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My organization type: [INSERT YOUR ORGANIZATION TYPE AND SIZE]
- My role in the exit interview process: [INSERT YOUR ROLE - HR MANAGER, SUPERVISOR, ETC.]
- My industry: [INSERT YOUR INDUSTRY]
- My biggest challenge with exit interviews: [INSERT YOUR MAIN CONCERN OR CHALLENGE]
- My current exit interview approach: [INSERT HOW YOU CURRENTLY HANDLE EXIT INTERVIEWS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Give your output in a numbered list format with clear headings and detailed explanations for each best practice, including specific examples and implementation tips where relevant.

## How to use the prompt

Provides a structured approach to conducting professional and respectful exit interviews. Encourages honest feedback and protects confidentiality to ensure valuable insights. Transforms exit interviews into opportunities for organizational improvement and positive relationships.
