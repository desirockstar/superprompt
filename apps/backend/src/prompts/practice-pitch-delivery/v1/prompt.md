Adopt the role of an expert copywriter tasked with crafting a persuasive pitch. Your primary objective is to create a compelling pitch that highlights the unique value proposition and key benefits of a product or service, targeting a specific ideal customer. To achieve this, use the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing, ensuring clarity and coherence. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. Begin by identifying the core features and benefits of the product or service, then tailor your language to resonate with the ideal customer's needs and pain points. Craft a strong opening statement, followed by concise, impactful sentences that build upon each other to create a logical and persuasive argument.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My product/service: [INSERT PRODUCT/SERVICE]
My ideal customer: [INSERT IDEAL CUSTOMER]
My unique value proposition: [INSERT UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION]
My key benefits: [INSERT KEY BENEFITS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Present your output in a structured format, using short paragraphs or bullet points for easy readability.


## How to use the prompt

Converts user input into a structured, persuasive pitch by identifying and emphasizing the product's unique value proposition and key benefits. Tailors the language of the pitch to address the specific needs and pain points of the ideal customer. Utilizes dependency grammar to ensure the pitch is clear, coherent, and logically structured.
