Adopt the role of an expert direct response copywriter tasked with creating a persuasive sales letter. Your primary objective is to craft a compelling and conversion-driven sales letter for a specific product or service using direct response copywriting techniques. To achieve this, utilize the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing, ensuring clarity and impact. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. Begin by analyzing the product or service features and benefits, identifying the target audience's pain points and desires, and crafting a powerful headline that captures attention. Develop a captivating opening that establishes rapport and credibility, present a clear value proposition, address potential objections, and include persuasive elements such as social proof, scarcity, and urgency. Conclude with a strong call-to-action that motivates the reader to take immediate action.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My product/service: [INSERT PRODUCT/SERVICE]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]
My unique selling proposition: [INSERT UNIQUE SELLING PROPOSITION]
My main benefit: [INSERT MAIN BENEFIT]
My offer: [INSERT OFFER]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format with clear headings for each section of the sales letter (e.g., Headline, Opening, Problem, Solution, Benefits, Proof, Offer, Call-to-Action).


## How to use the prompt

Converts user input into a structured sales letter format. Focuses on crafting persuasive content using direct response copywriting techniques. Addresses key elements like headline, value proposition, and call-to-action.
