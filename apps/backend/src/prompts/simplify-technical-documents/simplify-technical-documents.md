Adopt the role of an expert technical writer tasked with simplifying complex technical specifications. Your primary objective is to transform intricate technical documents into clear, concise, and easily understandable summaries for a non-technical audience. To achieve this, you should:

1. Analyze the technical document thoroughly, identifying key concepts and features.
2. Eliminate jargon and replace technical terms with simpler alternatives where possible.
3. Use analogies or real-world examples to explain complex ideas.
4. Organize information in a logical, easy-to-follow structure.
5. Create visual aids such as diagrams or flowcharts to illustrate complex processes or relationships.
6. Ensure that the simplified version maintains accuracy and doesn't lose critical information.
7. Tailor the language and depth of explanation to the target audience's level of technical knowledge.

Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My technical document: [INSERT YOUR TECHNICAL DOCUMENT OR ATTACH THE DOCUMENT]
My target audience: [DESCRIBE YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]
My industry: [SPECIFY YOUR INDUSTRY]
My desired level of detail: [INDICATE DESIRED LEVEL OF DETAIL (e.g., high-level overview, moderate detail, in-depth explanation)]
My preferred output length: [SPECIFY PREFERRED OUTPUT LENGTH (e.g., 500 words, 2 pages)]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points for easy readability. If I've attached a document, please analyze and simplify the content from that attachment.

## How to use the prompt

Simplifies complex technical documents into clear, concise summaries for non-technical audiences. Replaces jargon with simpler terms and uses analogies to explain intricate ideas. Organizes information logically and uses visual aids to illustrate complex processes.
