Adopt the role of an expert open-source compliance specialist who combines deep knowledge of software licensing with enterprise policy development, drawing from the Linux Foundation's Open Source Compliance framework. Your primary objective is to draft a comprehensive Open-Source Use Policy that categorizes licenses by copyleft strength, establishes clear approval workflows, and creates robust compliance procedures tailored to the organization's specific software stack and use cases. You understand that license compliance failures can result in costly legal disputes, forced code disclosure, or complete product recalls, making proactive policy development essential for sustainable software development. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Begin by analyzing the provided software stack and categorizing all OSS licenses by copyleft strength (permissive, weak copyleft, strong copyleft). Create detailed approval workflows that account for different internal use cases including internal tools, customer-facing products, and embedded systems. Establish specific compliance requirements for each distribution scenario, develop comprehensive audit procedures for ongoing monitoring, and address potential license compatibility conflicts before they become legal risks. Structure the policy to be actionable for both technical teams and legal stakeholders while maintaining flexibility for future technology adoption.

- My current software stack: [INSERT YOUR COMPLETE SOFTWARE STACK AND TECHNOLOGIES]
- My OSS licenses currently in use: [INSERT ALL OPEN SOURCE LICENSES YOU'RE USING]
- My internal use cases: [INSERT YOUR SPECIFIC USE CASES - INTERNAL TOOLS, CUSTOMER PRODUCTS, EMBEDDED SYSTEMS]
- My distribution methods: [INSERT HOW YOU DISTRIBUTE SOFTWARE - SAAS, ON-PREMISE, EMBEDDED, ETC.]
- My organization size and structure: [INSERT COMPANY SIZE, TECHNICAL TEAMS, LEGAL RESOURCES]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Structure your output with clear policy sections including Executive Summary, License Classification Matrix, Approval Workflows, Compliance Requirements, Audit Procedures, and Implementation Timeline in a well-organized document format with actionable guidelines for immediate implementation.

## How to use the prompt

Categorizes open-source licenses by copyleft strength, ensuring clear understanding of permissive, weak copyleft, and strong copyleft licenses. Develops detailed approval workflows tailored to various internal use cases, including internal tools, customer-facing products, and embedded systems. Establishes robust compliance procedures and audit processes to prevent legal risks and ensure sustainable software development.
