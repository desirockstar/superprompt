Adopt the role of an expert human resources strategist and organizational development consultant who specializes in performance management systems that bridge individual growth with strategic business outcomes. Your primary objective is to create 10 performance review questions that simultaneously evaluate employee contributions and catalyze their professional development in a comprehensive list format. You understand that effective performance reviews must move beyond generic assessments to become strategic conversations that align individual aspirations with organizational goals while identifying specific growth opportunities and actionable development paths. Design questions that encourage self-reflection, reveal hidden potential, assess strategic thinking capabilities, and uncover barriers to peak performance. Each question should serve dual purposes: providing managers with meaningful insights about employee performance while empowering employees to articulate their value, challenges, and career ambitions. Focus on creating questions that generate substantive dialogue rather than simple yes/no responses, incorporating elements of goal achievement, skill development, collaboration effectiveness, innovation contribution, and future readiness. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My industry/sector: [INSERT YOUR INDUSTRY OR BUSINESS SECTOR]
My company's strategic priorities: [INSERT YOUR ORGANIZATION'S KEY STRATEGIC GOALS]
My team size and structure: [INSERT DETAILS ABOUT YOUR TEAM COMPOSITION]
My performance review cycle: [INSERT HOW OFTEN YOU CONDUCT PERFORMANCE REVIEWS]
My biggest performance management challenges: [INSERT YOUR MAIN CHALLENGES WITH CURRENT REVIEW PROCESS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Give your output in a numbered list format with each question followed by a brief explanation of its strategic purpose and the type of insights it's designed to reveal.

## How to use the prompt

Develops performance review questions that evaluate employee contributions and foster professional growth. Aligns individual aspirations with organizational goals through strategic conversations. Encourages self-reflection, reveals potential, and assesses strategic thinking capabilities.
