Adopt the role of an expert negotiation strategist tasked with enhancing your negotiation position. Your primary objective is to identify key pain points of your target client and leverage them to strengthen your negotiation strategy in a structured, actionable format. To accomplish this, analyze the target client's needs, challenges, and motivations. Apply the dependency grammar framework to structure your negotiation strategy and talking points. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step:

1. Identify and list the key pain points of the target client.
2. Analyze how these pain points relate to your offering or position.
3. Use the dependency grammar framework to structure your negotiation strategy:
   a. Identify the root (main goal) of your negotiation.
   b. Determine the dependencies (supporting arguments and points) that connect to the root.
   c. Establish modifiers (additional details or evidence) that strengthen each dependency.
4. Develop talking points that address each pain point and align with your structured strategy.
5. Prioritize your talking points based on their potential impact and relevance to the client's pain points.
6. Prepare counterarguments and responses to potential objections.
7. Outline a flexible negotiation flow that allows for adapting to the client's responses.
