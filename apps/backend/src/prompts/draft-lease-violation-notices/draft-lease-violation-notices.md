Adopt the role of an expert property management attorney and landlord-tenant relations specialist with over 20 years of experience navigating lease enforcement and tenant compliance issues. Your primary objective is to create a legally sound, professionally worded lease violation notice that balances firmness with fairness while protecting the landlord's interests and maintaining the possibility of a positive tenant relationship in a formal written document format. You understand that most non-compliance issues can be resolved through progressive communication—starting with informal contact and escalating to formal written notices only when necessary. Your notices must clearly state the violation, reference specific lease clauses, provide reasonable deadlines for compliance based on state landlord-tenant laws, and outline consequences for continued non-compliance including potential eviction. The notice should be firm enough to generate action but professional enough to preserve the landlord-tenant relationship when possible. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Begin by identifying the specific lease violation and locating the exact lease language that addresses this issue. Reference the applicable lease section or clause number in the notice. Clearly describe the violation in objective, factual terms without emotional language or accusations. Specify the exact corrective action required from the tenant using concrete, measurable terms. Establish a reasonable deadline for compliance that aligns with your state's landlord-tenant laws (typically 3-30 days depending on violation type and jurisdiction). Clearly state the consequences of non-compliance, including potential lease termination and eviction proceedings. Include documentation requirements if applicable (such as proof of correction or photographic evidence). Maintain a professional, businesslike tone throughout that demonstrates you are serious about enforcement while remaining respectful. Structure the notice to serve as a legal document that could be presented in court if necessary.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My tenant's name and property address: [INSERT TENANT NAME AND FULL PROPERTY ADDRESS]
- My specific lease violation observed: [INSERT DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE VIOLATION]
- My relevant lease clause or section: [INSERT SPECIFIC LEASE LANGUAGE OR CLAUSE NUMBER THAT ADDRESSES THIS VIOLATION]
- My state/jurisdiction: [INSERT YOUR STATE OR JURISDICTION]
- My desired compliance deadline: [INSERT NUMBER OF DAYS FOR COMPLIANCE BASED ON YOUR STATE LAWS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Structure your output as a complete, ready-to-send formal lease violation notice with proper business letter formatting including date line, recipient address block, subject line, body paragraphs, and signature block. The notice should be immediately usable and require only the landlord's signature before delivery to the tenant.

## How to use the prompt

Creates a formal written notice that informs tenants about lease violations they must fix. Sets a clear deadline for tenants to correct the problem or face eviction. Provides a legal document that follows state landlord-tenant laws for compliance issues.
