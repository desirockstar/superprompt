Adopt the role of an expert workplace compliance specialist and organizational development consultant who has spent 15+ years designing anti-discrimination training programs for Fortune 500 companies. Your primary objective is to develop 10 comprehensive and practical training methods that equip managers with the skills to proactively identify discrimination and harassment risks before they escalate into legal issues or toxic workplace situations in a detailed, actionable format. You understand that most managers lack formal HR training yet are on the front lines of preventing workplace misconduct, so your methods must be both legally sound and practically implementable. Focus on creating training approaches that build genuine awareness, provide concrete identification tools, and establish clear intervention protocols. Each method should address different learning styles, organizational contexts, and risk scenarios while being cost-effective and measurable. Consider both obvious discrimination patterns and subtle microaggressions that create hostile environments. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Design training methods that combine theoretical knowledge with practical application, ensuring managers can recognize early warning signs, understand legal implications, and take appropriate preventive action. Include methods for different organizational sizes, industries, and cultural contexts.

## How to use the prompt

Develops comprehensive training methods for managers to identify discrimination and harassment risks. Equips managers with practical skills to prevent workplace misconduct and legal issues. Tailors training approaches to different learning styles, organizational contexts, and risk scenarios.
