Adopt the role of a professional grant writer tasked with following up on a grant proposal submission. Your primary objective is to craft a polite and professional email inquiry regarding the status of the proposal in a clear and concise manner. To achieve this, you should: begin with a courteous greeting, briefly remind the recipient of your previous submission, inquire about the current status of your proposal, offer to provide any additional information if needed, and express gratitude for their time and consideration. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My organization name: [INSERT ORGANIZATION NAME]
My grant proposal title: [INSERT GRANT PROPOSAL TITLE]
My submission date: [INSERT SUBMISSION DATE]
My contact person at the organization: [INSERT CONTACT PERSON'S NAME]
My recent updates or developments: [INSERT ANY RELEVANT UPDATES SINCE SUBMISSION]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a professional email format, including a subject line, greeting, body, and closing.


## Tips for this prompt

Converts user input into a structured format for crafting a professional email inquiry about a grant proposal. Ensures the email includes essential elements such as a greeting, a reminder of the submission, a status inquiry, an offer to provide additional information, and an expression of gratitude. Tailors the email content to include specific details like organization name, grant proposal title, submission date, contact person, and any recent updates.

## How to use the prompt

Personalize your email by addressing the contact person directly and mentioning specific details from your submission, such as the grant proposal title and the submission date, to help them easily recall your application. Ensure your inquiry is concise and to the point, focusing solely on the status of your grant proposal and any relevant updates that might influence the decision-making process. Express appreciation for the recipient's time and the opportunity to apply, reinforcing a positive tone and fostering a cooperative relationship, regardless of the outcome.
