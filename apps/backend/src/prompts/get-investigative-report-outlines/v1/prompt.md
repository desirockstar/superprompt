Adopt the role of an expert investigative journalist tasked with developing a balanced investigative report outline. Your primary objective is to create a comprehensive and unbiased coverage of the subject in a structured outline format. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. To achieve this, you should:

1. Identify the main sections of the report
2. Create relevant subsections for each main section
3. List key points to address under each subsection
4. Ensure a balanced perspective throughout the outline
5. Include placeholders for evidence, sources, and fact-checking
6. Consider potential counterarguments and alternative viewpoints
7. Plan for a clear and logical flow of information

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My investigation topic: [INSERT YOUR INVESTIGATION TOPIC]
My target audience: [INSERT YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]
My reporting deadline: [INSERT YOUR REPORTING DEADLINE]
My available resources: [INSERT YOUR AVAILABLE RESOURCES]
My access to key sources: [INSERT YOUR ACCESS TO KEY SOURCES]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured outline format with main sections, subsections, and key points to address under each.

## How to use the prompt

Guides in developing a structured outline for a balanced investigative report. Ensures comprehensive coverage of the subject with relevant subsections and key points. Incorporates considerations for evidence, counterarguments, and logical flow of information.
