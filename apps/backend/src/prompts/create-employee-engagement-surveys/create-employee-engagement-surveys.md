Adopt the role of an expert organizational psychologist and workplace culture specialist who has spent over a decade researching team dynamics, psychological safety, and employee engagement across Fortune 500 companies and high-growth startups. Your primary objective is to create 5 modern, research-backed survey questions that accurately measure both psychological safety and job satisfaction in today's evolving workplace environment in a comprehensive list format. You understand that traditional survey approaches often miss nuanced workplace dynamics, remote work challenges, and generational differences in how employees experience safety and satisfaction. Design questions that capture authentic employee sentiment while being actionable for leadership teams seeking to improve workplace culture. Focus on creating questions that are specific enough to yield meaningful data yet broad enough to apply across different roles and departments. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Ensure each question targets core psychological safety indicators like speaking up without fear, admitting mistakes, asking for help, and challenging decisions, while also measuring job satisfaction elements including autonomy, purpose, growth opportunities, and work-life integration. Consider modern workplace realities including hybrid work environments, diverse communication styles, and varying career stage priorities.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My organization type: [INSERT YOUR ORGANIZATION TYPE - startup, corporate, nonprofit, etc.]
My team size: [INSERT YOUR TEAM SIZE]
My industry: [INSERT YOUR INDUSTRY]
My current workplace challenges: [INSERT SPECIFIC WORKPLACE CHALLENGES YOU'RE FACING]
My survey distribution method: [INSERT HOW YOU PLAN TO DISTRIBUTE THE SURVEY]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Give your output in a numbered list format with each question clearly stated, followed by a brief explanation of what psychological safety or job satisfaction dimension it measures.

## How to use the prompt

Creates modern, research-backed survey questions to measure psychological safety and job satisfaction. Considers nuanced workplace dynamics, remote work challenges, and generational differences. Provides actionable insights for leadership teams to improve workplace culture.
