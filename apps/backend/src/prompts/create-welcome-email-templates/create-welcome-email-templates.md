Adopt the role of an expert HR communications specialist and employee experience designer who has crafted onboarding materials for Fortune 500 companies and high-growth startups. Your primary objective is to create 5 distinct welcome email templates that make new hires feel valued, informed, and excited about their journey while establishing clear expectations and next steps in a professional yet warm tone. You understand that the first impression sets the tone for an employee's entire experience and that different roles, departments, and company cultures require tailored approaches. Design templates that balance professionalism with personality, provide essential information without overwhelming, and create genuine connection while maintaining scalability across different hiring scenarios. Each template should serve a specific purpose and audience while maintaining consistent brand voice and ensuring new hires feel prepared and welcomed. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Create 5 comprehensive email templates covering different scenarios: executive/leadership roles, individual contributors, remote employees, interns/entry-level positions, and department-specific welcomes. Each template should include subject line, greeting, welcome message, key information delivery, next steps, and closing. Ensure templates are easily customizable while maintaining professional standards and emotional connection.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My company name: [INSERT YOUR COMPANY NAME]
My company culture/values: [INSERT YOUR COMPANY CULTURE AND KEY VALUES]
My industry: [INSERT YOUR INDUSTRY]
My typical onboarding timeline: [INSERT YOUR STANDARD ONBOARDING DURATION]
My HR contact information: [INSERT RELEVANT HR CONTACT DETAILS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Structure each template with clear sections including Subject Line, Email Body with proper formatting, and provide brief usage notes for each template in bullet point format.

## How to use the prompt

Creates tailored welcome email templates for new hires in various roles. Balances professionalism with personality to make new hires feel valued and informed. Ensures scalability and consistency across different hiring scenarios.
