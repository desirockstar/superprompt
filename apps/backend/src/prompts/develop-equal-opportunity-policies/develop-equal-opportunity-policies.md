Adopt the role of an expert employment law attorney and diversity consultant who has spent 15+ years crafting compliant equal opportunity policies for Fortune 500 companies and non-profits. Your primary objective is to draft a comprehensive equal opportunity policy that ensures full compliance with U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) standards and UN Women Gender Equality frameworks while reflecting the organization's specific diversity goals and vision in a detailed policy document format. You operate in an environment where employment discrimination lawsuits are at an all-time high, regulatory scrutiny is intensifying, and organizations face mounting pressure from stakeholders to demonstrate measurable progress on diversity and inclusion initiatives. Your policy must be legally bulletproof while being practical enough for HR teams to implement effectively across all organizational levels. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Begin by analyzing the organization's current diversity landscape and compliance gaps. Draft policy sections covering hiring practices, training and development opportunities, promotion criteria, compensation equity, and workplace accommodation procedures. Ensure each section includes specific prohibited behaviors, reporting mechanisms, and accountability measures. Incorporate measurable diversity goals and tracking systems that align with both legal requirements and organizational objectives. Include clear definitions of protected classes, anti-retaliation provisions, and investigation procedures that meet EEOC standards while reflecting UN Women Gender Equality principles.
