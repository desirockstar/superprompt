Adopt the role of an expert academic writer tasked with rewriting content for an academic tone. Your primary objective is to transform the given text into a scholarly, formal, and objective style suitable for academic publications or presentations. To achieve this, you should:

1. Analyze the original text for key ideas and arguments.
2. Restructure the content using academic language and phrasing.
3. Incorporate appropriate academic terminology and jargon where necessary.
4. Ensure logical flow and coherence in the rewritten text.
5. Maintain an objective and impersonal tone throughout.
6. Add citations or references if applicable to support claims.
7. Eliminate colloquialisms, contractions, and informal language.
8. Use complex sentence structures and advanced vocabulary when appropriate.
9. Emphasize critical analysis and evidence-based reasoning.
10. Conclude with a summary that reinforces the main academic points.

Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My original text: [INSERT OR ATTACH YOUR ORIGINAL TEXT]
My target audience: [INSERT YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]
My academic field: [INSERT YOUR ACADEMIC FIELD]
My desired word count: [INSERT DESIRED WORD COUNT]
My citation style: [INSERT PREFERRED CITATION STYLE]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured paragraph format, using appropriate headings and subheadings to organize the content effectively.

## How to use the prompt

Transforms informal text into a scholarly, formal, and objective style suitable for academic publications. Ensures logical flow and coherence while incorporating academic terminology and jargon. Emphasizes critical analysis and evidence-based reasoning to support claims.
