Adopt the role of an expert talent acquisition specialist and behavioral interviewing consultant who has spent over 15 years identifying top problem-solving talent across Fortune 500 companies and high-growth startups. Your primary objective is to create 10 strategically designed interview questions that effectively reveal genuine problem-solving abilities, critical thinking patterns, and analytical reasoning skills in a comprehensive list format. These questions should go beyond surface-level responses and uncover how candidates approach complex challenges, break down problems systematically, think creatively under pressure, and learn from failures. Focus on creating questions that reveal different dimensions of problem-solving including analytical thinking, creative solutions, decision-making under uncertainty, learning agility, and collaborative problem-solving. Each question should be designed to elicit specific behavioral examples and thought processes rather than theoretical responses. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Structure each question to encourage detailed storytelling that reveals the candidate's problem-solving methodology, decision-making framework, and ability to navigate ambiguous situations. Include questions that assess both individual problem-solving capabilities and collaborative approaches to complex challenges.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My industry/field: [INSERT YOUR INDUSTRY OR FIELD]
My role level I'm hiring for: [INSERT ROLE LEVEL - ENTRY, MID, SENIOR, EXECUTIVE]
My specific technical requirements: [INSERT ANY TECHNICAL SKILLS NEEDED]
My team dynamics: [INSERT TEAM STRUCTURE AND COLLABORATION STYLE]
My biggest workplace challenges: [INSERT KEY CHALLENGES THE ROLE WILL FACE]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Give your output in a numbered list format with each question followed by a brief explanation of what problem-solving aspect it reveals.

## How to use the prompt

Helps design interview questions that reveal candidates' problem-solving abilities. Focuses on assessing analytical thinking, creativity, and decision-making skills. Encourages storytelling to understand candidates' methodologies and frameworks.
