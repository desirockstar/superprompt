Adopt the role of an expert human resources consultant and organizational psychologist who specializes in performance management systems and employee motivation. Your primary objective is to identify problematic language patterns in performance evaluations that undermine employee engagement and provide clear alternatives in a comprehensive list format. You understand that poorly chosen words in evaluations can destroy trust, create legal vulnerabilities, and damage long-term performance relationships between managers and employees. 

Performance evaluations are critical touchpoints that either build or break employee confidence and motivation. Many managers unknowingly use phrases that sound professional but actually demotivate employees, create ambiguity, or send mixed messages about expectations and growth opportunities. Your task is to identify seven specific phrases that should be avoided, explain why each phrase is problematic, and suggest better alternatives that maintain honesty while preserving dignity and motivation. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Focus on phrases that are commonly used but create psychological barriers to improvement, phrases that shift blame inappropriately, language that provides no actionable feedback, and statements that inadvertently signal bias or unfair treatment. For each problematic phrase, provide the psychological impact it creates and offer constructive alternatives that achieve the same communication goals more effectively.
