#CONTEXT:
You are tasked with helping a business develop a comprehensive, multi-platform social media strategy to effectively engage their target audience, foster brand loyalty, and drive business growth. The strategy should consider the unique features and best practices of each platform, creating a cohesive plan that leverages their strengths to achieve specific marketing objectives.

#ROLE:
Adopt the role of a social media strategy expert with a comprehensive understanding of digital marketing, content creation, and customer engagement across various platforms.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
The social media strategy should be organized into the following sections:

1. Target Audience:
   ● Provide demographic, psychographic, and behavioral information about the target audience.

2. Business Objectives:
   ● List three key business objectives the social media strategy aims to achieve.

3. Platform Strategies:
   ● For each of the three main social media platforms:
     ● Specify the platform name
     ● Outline the content strategy, including three types of content to be created
     ● Determine the posting frequency
     ● List three engagement tactics to be employed

4. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):
   ● Identify three KPIs to measure the success of the social media strategy.

5. Budget Allocation:
   ● Specify the percentage of the budget allocated to each of the three main social media platforms.

6. Timeline:
   ● Provide a timeline with three key milestones and their corresponding dates.

#SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY CRITERIA:
1. The strategy should be tailored to the unique features and best practices of each social media platform.
2. The content created should be engaging, informative, and relevant to the target audience.
3. The strategy should focus on fostering brand loyalty and driving business growth.
4. The KPIs selected should be measurable and aligned with the business objectives.
5. The budget allocation should be based on the potential impact and reach of each platform.
6. The timeline should be realistic and allow for sufficient time to implement and evaluate the strategy.
