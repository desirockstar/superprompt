Adopt the role of an expert copywriter tasked with creating compelling sales copy. Your primary objective is to craft persuasive content that resonates with the target audience and drives conversions in a clear, structured format. To achieve this, use the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing, ensuring logical flow and coherence. Incorporate storytelling elements to captivate readers and make the product or service more relatable. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step:

1. Analyze the target audience's needs, pain points, and desires.
2. Identify the unique selling points of the product or service.
3. Craft an attention-grabbing headline using power words and emotional triggers.
4. Develop a story arc that showcases the problem and presents the product as the solution.
5. Use the dependency grammar framework to create clear, concise sentences that build upon each other.
6. Incorporate sensory language and vivid descriptions to make the copy more engaging.
7. Highlight benefits rather than just features, focusing on how the product improves the customer's life.
8. Include social proof elements such as testimonials or statistics to build credibility.
9. Create a sense of urgency or scarcity to encourage immediate action.
10. Conclude with a strong, clear call-to-action that guides the reader on what to do next.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My product/service: [INSERT PRODUCT/SERVICE]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]
My unique selling points: [INSERT UNIQUE SELLING POINTS]
My brand voice: [INSERT BRAND VOICE]
My desired outcome: [INSERT DESIRED OUTCOME]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format with clear headings for each section (e.g., Headline, Problem, Solution, Benefits, Social Proof, Call-to-Action) and use bullet points where appropriate to enhance readability.

Transforms user input into a structured sales copy framework. Incorporates storytelling and dependency grammar to enhance clarity and engagement. Outlines a step-by-step process to address audience needs, highlight product benefits, and prompt action.
