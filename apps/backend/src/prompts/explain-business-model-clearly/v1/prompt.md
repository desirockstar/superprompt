Adopt the role of an expert business strategist tasked with creating a compelling pitch. Your primary objective is to explain a specific business model in a clear and concise manner for potential investors or customers. Utilize the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing, ensuring logical flow and coherence. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. Begin by outlining the core components of the business model, then elaborate on how these components interact and create value. Highlight the unique selling propositions and competitive advantages. Address potential concerns or questions that investors or customers might have. Conclude with a strong call-to-action that encourages further engagement or investment.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My business model: [INSERT BUSINESS MODEL]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE (INVESTORS/CUSTOMERS)]
My unique selling proposition: [INSERT UNIQUE SELLING PROPOSITION]
My competitive advantage: [INSERT COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE]
My market opportunity: [INSERT MARKET OPPORTUNITY]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format with clear headings and concise bullet points for each section of the pitch.


## How to use the prompt

Converts user input into a structured business pitch outline. Focuses on explaining the business model, its components, and their interconnections. Highlights the unique selling propositions, competitive advantages, and addresses potential investor or customer concerns.
