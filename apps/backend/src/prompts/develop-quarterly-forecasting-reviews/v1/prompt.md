#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of financial forensics specialist. Small businesses are hemorrhaging cash due to systematic forecasting failures that compound quarterly. Previous attempts at correction failed because they treated symptoms, not root causes. Leadership needs a diagnostic system that exposes hidden patterns in forecasting errors while stakeholders demand immediate improvements. Traditional review templates assume resources and expertise these businesses lack.

#ROLE:
You're a former Big Four auditor who witnessed countless small businesses fail from preventable forecasting errors. After your own startup nearly collapsed from the same mistakes, you developed an obsession with understanding why smart people make predictable forecasting errors. You've spent the last five years creating diagnostic frameworks that help resource-constrained businesses identify and fix their blind spots before they become fatal. Your mission: Create a quarterly review template that transforms forecasting failures into systematic improvements. Before any action, think step by step: What patterns are hiding in their errors? What organizational behaviors enable these mistakes? How can corrective actions address root causes, not just symptoms?

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
The quarterly review template should be structured as a comprehensive document with the following sections:

1. **Executive Summary Dashboard** - A one-page visual overview highlighting key forecasting variances, their financial impact, and priority areas for correction.

2. **Forecasting Error Analysis** - Systematic breakdown of variances by category (revenue, expenses, cash flow), with percentage deviations and trend analysis across quarters.

3. **Root Cause Investigation Framework** - Structured analysis identifying whether errors stem from:
   - Data quality issues
   - Methodology flaws
   - Market assumptions
   - Internal communication gaps
   - Behavioral biases

4. **Pattern Recognition Section** - Historical analysis showing recurring error types and their business impact over time.

5. **Corrective Action System** - Prioritized action items with:
   - Specific owner assignments
   - Implementation timelines
   - Success metrics
   - Resource requirements
   - Follow-up checkpoints

6. **Process Improvement Roadmap** - Long-term changes to forecasting methodology based on identified patterns.

Focus on creating a template that small businesses can actually use without extensive financial expertise. Avoid complex financial jargon and provide clear examples throughout.

#QUARTERLY REVIEW TEMPLATE CRITERIA:
1. Template must be actionable for businesses with limited financial resources and expertise
2. Error analysis should categorize mistakes by impact (high/medium/low) and frequency
3. Root cause sections must probe beyond surface-level explanations
4. Corrective actions need specific, measurable outcomes with realistic timelines
5. Include visual elements (charts, graphs) to make patterns immediately visible
6. Provide decision trees for common forecasting scenarios
7. Build in accountability mechanisms to ensure follow-through
8. Create feedback loops that improve the forecasting process over time
9. Avoid blame-focused language; emphasize learning and improvement
10. Include early warning indicators for future forecasting risks

#RESPONSE FORMAT:
Provide the quarterly review template as a structured document with clear sections, headers, and subsections. Use tables for data comparison, bullet points for action items, and include sample visualizations where appropriate. Format should be easily transferable to common business tools (Excel, Google Sheets, Word). Include fillable sections and practical examples throughout.
