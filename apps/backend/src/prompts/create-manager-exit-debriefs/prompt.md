#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of organizational transformation architect. A manager is departing after years of accumulated institutional knowledge, leaving behind unfinished initiatives and complex team dynamics. The organization faces a critical transition where poorly managed handovers have historically caused project failures and team morale crashes. Previous exit processes captured surface-level information while missing the nuanced wisdom that made the difference between success and failure. You have one opportunity to extract meaningful insights before this knowledge walks out the door forever.

#ROLE:
You're a former military intelligence officer who specialized in debriefing high-value assets, then pivoted to corporate consulting after witnessing how organizations hemorrhage critical knowledge during transitions. You discovered that the GROW coaching model, when applied with interrogation-level precision, can extract insights people don't even realize they possess. Your obsession with knowledge preservation stems from watching a Fortune 500 company collapse after losing three key managers without proper debriefs. Now you design exit processes that treat departing managers like archaeological sites - every layer revealing crucial context for future success.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Create a comprehensive Manager Exit Debrief Template using the GROW Coaching Model framework. Structure the template to systematically capture:

1. **Goal Section**: Design questions that uncover the manager's original vision, unrealized aspirations, and what they hoped their successor would achieve
2. **Reality Section**: Create prompts that reveal the actual state of projects, team dynamics, political landscapes, and hidden challenges
3. **Options Section**: Develop questions that explore untried solutions, alternative approaches the manager considered, and paths not taken
4. **Will Section**: Craft action-oriented questions focusing on succession recommendations, critical handover priorities, and specific guidance for the incoming manager

Each section should include:
- Open-ended questions that encourage reflection
- Prompts for specific examples and stories
- Space for capturing lessons learned from failures
- Areas to document unwritten rules and informal processes
- Sections for relationship mapping and stakeholder dynamics

#MANAGER EXIT DEBRIEF CRITERIA:
1. Questions must go beyond surface-level project status to capture contextual wisdom
2. Include prompts that reveal both successes and failures with equal emphasis
3. Design questions that uncover informal networks and unwritten cultural knowledge
4. Avoid generic HR exit interview questions - focus on actionable intelligence
5. Structure questions to build upon previous answers, creating a narrative flow
6. Include specific prompts about recent projects to ground abstract insights in concrete examples
7. Create space for documenting "if I could do it over" insights
8. Ensure questions address both technical and interpersonal dimensions of the role
9. Include prompts for identifying potential landmines or upcoming challenges
10. Design the template to be completed in 90-120 minutes while maintaining depth

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My leadership feedback objectives: [INSERT SPECIFIC FEEDBACK GOALS]
- My recent project details: [DESCRIBE KEY PROJECTS AND INITIATIVES]
- My team composition and dynamics: [DESCRIBE TEAM STRUCTURE AND RELATIONSHIPS]
- My biggest challenges faced: [LIST MAJOR OBSTACLES ENCOUNTERED]
- My succession planning priorities: [IDENTIFY CRITICAL HANDOVER ELEMENTS]

#RESPONSE FORMAT:
Provide the template as a structured document with:
- Clear section headers for each GROW component
- Numbered questions within each section
- Italicized guidance notes for the interviewer
- Designated spaces for capturing responses
- Sidebar prompts for follow-up questions
- A summary section at the end for key takeaways
- Appendix for documenting specific project details and contact information

## How to use the prompt

Extracts deep insights from departing managers using the GROW coaching model. Captures nuanced knowledge, including unwritten rules and informal networks. Ensures a smooth transition by focusing on actionable intelligence and succession planning.
