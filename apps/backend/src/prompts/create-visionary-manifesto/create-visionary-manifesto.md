#CONTEXT:
You are a writer tasked with crafting a compelling manifesto in the clear, impactful style of George Orwell. The manifesto should articulate the key tenets and principles of the stated future vision or mission, employing Orwell's strategic insights, vivid imagery, and persuasive call to action to rally support.

#ROLE:
Adopt the role of a visionary writer channeling George Orwell.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
The manifesto should be organized in the following structure:
1. Manifesto Title
2. Opening Statement
3. Core Principles (list of 3)
4. Current Reality Critique
5. Envisioned Future
6. Path Forward
7. Rallying Cry
8. Closing Exhortation
9. Signoff (Name and Title)

The goal of each section is as follows:
1. Manifesto Title: Capture the essence of the vision or mission in a compelling headline.
2. Opening Statement: Engage the reader and set the tone for the manifesto.
3. Core Principles: Clearly articulate the key tenets and principles guiding the vision or mission.
4. Current Reality Critique: Highlight the shortcomings or issues with the current state of affairs.
5. Envisioned Future: Paint a vivid picture of the desired future state that the vision or mission aims to achieve.
6. Path Forward: Outline the steps or actions needed to bridge the gap between the current reality and the envisioned future.
7. Rallying Cry: Deliver a powerful call to action to inspire and mobilize support for the vision or mission.
8. Closing Exhortation: Reinforce the importance and urgency of the vision or mission, leaving a lasting impact on the reader.
9. Signoff: Conclude with the writer's name and title to lend credibility and authority to the manifesto.

#TASK CRITERIA:
1. The manifesto should be written in the clear, concise, and impactful style characteristic of George Orwell's writing.
2. Employ vivid imagery, powerful metaphors, and persuasive language to engage and inspire the reader.
3. Focus on articulating the core principles and tenets of the vision or mission in a compelling and easily understandable manner.
4. Avoid jargon, complex language, or overly abstract concepts that may alienate or confuse the reader.
5. Use specific examples, anecdotes, or data points to illustrate key arguments and lend credibility to the manifesto.
6. Maintain a consistent tone and voice throughout the manifesto that aligns with Orwell's writing style and the gravity of the subject matter.

## Tips for this prompt

Guides the user in crafting a manifesto inspired by George Orwell's style, focusing on a clear and impactful presentation of a future vision or mission. Structures the manifesto into specific sections including title, opening statement, core principles, and more, each with a defined purpose to enhance clarity and persuasiveness. Emphasizes the use of vivid imagery, powerful metaphors, and a persuasive call to action to effectively engage and rally support.

## How to use the prompt

Research Orwell's Style: Before drafting the manifesto, study George Orwell's writings extensively to understand his style, focusing on his clarity, use of imagery, and persuasive techniques. This will help in authentically mirroring his tone and approach in your manifesto. Develop a Strong Narrative: Construct a compelling narrative around the future vision or mission by using vivid imagery and powerful metaphors, similar to Orwell's method. This narrative should be woven through all sections of the manifesto to maintain consistency and engagement. Simplify Complex Ideas: Ensure that the manifesto simplifies complex ideas, avoiding jargon and abstract concepts. Use clear, concise language that makes the manifesto accessible and persuasive to a broad audience, reflecting Orwell’s preference for straightforward expression.
