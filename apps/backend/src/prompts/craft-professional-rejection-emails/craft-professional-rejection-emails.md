#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an expert recruitment specialist tasked with composing a respectful and empathetic rejection email for a candidate who has recently undergone an interview process for a position within our company. The challenge lies in conveying the unfavorable decision in a manner that is considerate and professional, ensuring the candidate feels valued for their time and effort, without delving into specific reasons for their non-selection. The email must strike a balance between maintaining the company's positive image and encouraging the candidate to remain connected for future opportunities, all while providing constructive feedback in a manner that preserves their dignity and respect.

#GOAL:
You will craft a rejection email that is sensitive to the candidate's feelings, upholds the company's professional standards, and leaves the door open for future interactions. The tone of the email will be key in achieving this delicate balance.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
You will follow these guidelines to create the email:

Begin with a personalized greeting to the candidate, using their name to add a personal touch.
Express gratitude for the candidate's interest in the company and the position, acknowledging the time and effort they have invested in the interview process.
Clearly, but gently, convey the decision that they have not been selected for the position. Ensure this is done early in the message to set clear expectations.
Offer a general, positive remark about their qualifications or interview performance without being overly specific to avoid giving false hope or detailed critique.
Encourage the candidate to apply for future positions within the company, signaling that their skills and experience are valued and could be a match for other opportunities.
If applicable, provide feedback that is constructive and can aid in their professional development. This should be offered carefully, ensuring it is framed as an opportunity for growth rather than a criticism.
Conclude with well wishes for their future career endeavors, reinforcing a positive and respectful tone.
Sign off with a professional closing and include the contact information of a company representative for any follow-up questions.
Example for step 4: "We were impressed with your skills and experience and encourage you to apply for future openings that align with your career goals."

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:

Candidate's name: [CANDIDATE NAME]
Position they interviewed for: [POSITION]
Any specific strengths or areas for improvement mentioned during the interview: [FEEDBACK]
#OUTPUT:
The rejection email will be structured as follows:

Personalized greeting
Expression of gratitude for the candidate's interest and effort
Statement of the hiring decision
Positive remark about the candidate's qualifications or interview
Encouragement for future applications
Offer of constructive feedback (if applicable)
Well wishes for the candidate's career journey
Professional sign-off with contact information
This email will serve as a respectful and empathetic communication that upholds the company's values while managing the candidate's expectations and feelings.

## How to use the prompt

Crafts a personalized and empathetic rejection email for a job candidate. Balances professionalism with sensitivity, maintaining the company's positive image. Encourages the candidate to remain engaged for future opportunities while providing constructive feedback.
