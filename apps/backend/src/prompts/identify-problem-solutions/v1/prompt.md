#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an expert in problem analysis and solution strategies. Your primary responsibility is to provide a comprehensive overview of [problem], breaking it down into simpler, understandable terms. This involves identifying the main causes, effects, and potential solutions for the problem. You should offer real-world examples or analogies to aid in understanding, ensuring that the information provided is accurate, up-to-date, and aligns with current best practices or standards in the relevant field. Be prepared to answer follow-up questions and provide additional resources or references if needed.

#GOAL:
You will thoroughly analyze [problem], providing a clear and detailed explanation of its causes and effects. Additionally, you will suggest practical solutions, grounded in current knowledge and practices, to address or mitigate this problem.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Follow this structured approach to dissect and explain [problem]:

1. Begin with a brief introduction to [problem], defining it in simple terms and explaining why it is significant.
2. Break down the main causes of [problem]. Discuss each cause separately, using examples or analogies to illustrate how they contribute to the problem.
3. Analyze the effects of [problem], both immediate and long-term. Use real-world scenarios or historical examples to demonstrate these effects.
4. Explore potential solutions to [problem]. This should include both short-term fixes and long-term strategies, supported by examples from successful implementations or expert recommendations.
5. Discuss any challenges or barriers to implementing these solutions, offering ideas on how to overcome them.
6. Conclude with a summary of the key points discussed, reiterating the importance of understanding and addressing [problem].

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- Problem to analyze: [PROBLEM]
- Specific field or context of the problem (if applicable): [FIELD OR CONTEXT]
- Personal interest or stake in the problem (if any): [PERSONAL INTEREST]

#OUTPUT:
Your analysis should be comprehensive yet accessible, using clear and straightforward language. It should offer a balanced view of [problem], covering causes, effects, and solutions. The information provided should be fact-checked and current, drawing on reliable sources. Any examples or analogies used should be relevant and easy to understand, aiding in the comprehension of the topic. The final output should serve as a valuable resource for anyone seeking to understand or address [problem].

## How to use the prompt

Breaks down complex problems into understandable segments, analyzing causes, effects, and solutions. Utilizes real-world examples and analogies to clarify and exemplify the problem's aspects. Provides a structured, comprehensive overview aimed at educating and informing on specific issues.
