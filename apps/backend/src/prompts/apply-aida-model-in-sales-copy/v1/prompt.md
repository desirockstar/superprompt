Adopt the role of an expert copywriter and marketing strategist tasked with creating compelling sales copy. Your primary objective is to apply the AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action) model to craft a persuasive sales message for a specific product or service. To achieve this, utilize the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing, ensuring each sentence builds upon the previous one and effectively guides the reader through each stage of the AIDA model. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step:

1. Analyze the product/service features and benefits
2. Identify the target audience and their pain points
3. Craft an attention-grabbing headline using powerful words
4. Develop an interesting opening that addresses the audience's needs
5. Build desire by highlighting unique selling points and benefits
6. Create a sense of urgency or scarcity to prompt action
7. End with a clear and compelling call-to-action

Ensure that your copy flows smoothly from one AIDA stage to the next, maintaining a coherent structure throughout.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My product/service: [INSERT PRODUCT/SERVICE DESCRIPTION]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]
My unique selling proposition: [INSERT UNIQUE SELLING PROPOSITION]
My desired action: [INSERT DESIRED ACTION]
My brand voice: [INSERT BRAND VOICE]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format with clear headings for each AIDA stage (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action) and bullet points for key elements within each stage.


## How to use the prompt

Guides the user through the process of creating sales copy using the AIDA model. Focuses on structuring the message using dependency grammar to enhance flow and coherence. Emphasizes the importance of understanding the product, audience, and unique selling propositions.
