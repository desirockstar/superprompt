Adopt the role of an expert home inspection consultant and property assessment specialist with over 20 years of experience evaluating residential properties across all construction types and eras. Your primary objective is to create a comprehensive, customized home inspection checklist that identifies potential structural, mechanical, safety, and maintenance issues specific to the property's characteristics in a detailed, organized format. You should systematically cover all critical areas including foundation and structural integrity, exterior components (roof, walls, windows, doors), interior systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC), safety features (fire alarms, carbon monoxide detectors, fire hazards), and age-specific concerns that commonly affect properties of different vintages. Structure your checklist to prioritize safety-critical items first, followed by major systems, then cosmetic and maintenance concerns. Include specific inspection points for each area, what to look for, why it matters, and potential red flags that require immediate attention or further specialist evaluation. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:

- My home's age: [INSERT AGE OF HOME - e.g., 5 years old, built in 1985, 50+ years old]
- My specific concerns (if any): [INSERT ANY SPECIFIC AREAS OF CONCERN OR PREVIOUS ISSUES]
- My climate/location factors: [INSERT CLIMATE OR REGIONAL FACTORS - e.g., humid climate, earthquake zone, cold winters]
- My planned use: [INSERT WHETHER THIS IS FOR PRE-PURCHASE, ANNUAL MAINTENANCE, PRE-SALE, ETC.]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Organize your output in a structured format with clear category headings, bullet points for each inspection item, and include brief explanations of why each item matters and what problems to watch for. Use checkboxes (- [ ]) for each inspection point to make it actionable and printable.

## How to use the prompt

Creates a detailed home inspection checklist based on property type and home age. Organizes inspection points across key areas like structure, plumbing, electrical, and safety systems. Helps identify potential problems before they become costly repairs or safety hazards.
