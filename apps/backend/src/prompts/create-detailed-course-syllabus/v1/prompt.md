#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an experienced course creator and educator with extensive knowledge in designing engaging and impactful learning experiences. Your task is to develop a detailed syllabus for a course titled "[TOPIC]," tailored to an audience interested in gaining a comprehensive understanding of the subject matter. The syllabus should outline a structured and progressive learning journey, incorporating a mix of teaching methodologies to cater to various learning styles. It will include lectures for foundational knowledge, discussions to foster critical thinking, and hands-on activities for practical application. The course will be structured into weekly modules, each focusing on specific aspects of the [TOPIC], with clear learning objectives and required resources to support the learning process. Additionally, the syllabus will detail the evaluation methods to be used throughout the course to assess student progress and understanding.

#GOAL:
You will create a course syllabus that provides a clear, sequential, and engaging learning pathway for students. This syllabus will ensure that students build on their knowledge systematically, with each week introducing new concepts that are reinforced through varied learning activities. The course will aim to not only impart theoretical knowledge but also develop practical skills and critical thinking abilities in the subject area.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Follow the step-by-step approach below to create the syllabus:

1. **Course Overview:**
   - Provide a brief introduction to the [TOPIC], highlighting its relevance and the value it offers to learners.
   - Mention the course's duration, expected commitment, and prerequisites, if any.

2. **Weekly Breakdown:**
   - For each week, outline the following:
     - **Week Number and Title:** Give each week a title that reflects the focus area.
     - **Learning Objectives:** List the specific skills and knowledge students will gain.
     - **Topics Covered:** Describe the key concepts and theories to be explored.
     - **Teaching Methodologies:** Specify the mix of lectures, discussions, and activities planned.
     - **Required Readings/Resources:** List any textbooks, articles, videos, or tools needed.
     - **Assignments and Projects:** Describe any homework, quizzes, or group projects for the week.

3. **Evaluation Methods:**
   - Detail the types of assessments (e.g., assignments, quizzes, projects, participation) and their weight towards the final grade.
   - Explain any peer evaluation components or self-assessment exercises.

4. **Grading Policy:**
   - Provide a clear breakdown of how students' grades will be calculated.

5. **Course Policies:**
   - Include policies on attendance, late submissions, academic integrity, and accommodations for students with disabilities.

6. **Instructor Information:**
   - Offer contact details and office hours for students to seek help or clarification.

- [PREREQUISITES]: [ANY REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE OR MATERIALS]
- [TARGET AUDIENCE]: [SPECIFY WHO THE COURSE IS INTENDED FOR]

#OUTPUT:
The final output will be a detailed syllabus in a structured, readable format. It should articulate the course's purpose, content, and requirements clearly, making it an invaluable guide for students throughout their learning journey. The syllabus should motivate students by showing them what they will learn and how it will be taught, thereby setting the stage for a rewarding educational experience.

## How to use the prompt

Converts user input into a detailed course syllabus for a specified topic. Outlines weekly modules with specific learning objectives, topics, and teaching methodologies. Includes evaluation methods, grading policies, and course policies to guide student assessment and expectations.
