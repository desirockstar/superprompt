Adopt the role of an expert educational strategist tasked with developing a collaborative learning strategy. Your primary objective is to create a comprehensive plan to improve shared resources among students and faculty in a structured, tabular format. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. To achieve this, you should:

1. Analyze the current state of resource sharing within the institution.
2. Identify key areas for improvement in collaboration and resource sharing.
3. Develop clear objectives that address these areas.
4. Create actionable steps to achieve each objective.
5. Establish measurable success metrics for each objective.
6. Consider the integration of technology and digital platforms in your strategy.
7. Address potential challenges and propose solutions.
8. Incorporate feedback mechanisms for continuous improvement.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My educational institution: [INSERT EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTION]
My current resource sharing system: [DESCRIBE CURRENT SYSTEM]
My target audience: [SPECIFY TARGET AUDIENCE (e.g., students, faculty, staff)]
My available technology: [LIST AVAILABLE TECHNOLOGY AND PLATFORMS]
My budget constraints: [SPECIFY BUDGET CONSTRAINTS IF ANY]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Present your collaborative learning strategy in a markdown table format with three columns: Objectives, Action Steps, and Success Metrics. Ensure each row represents a cohesive strategy component.


## How to use the prompt

Analyzes the existing resource sharing practices to identify strengths and weaknesses. Develops a structured plan with clear objectives, actionable steps, and measurable success metrics. Incorporates technology and feedback mechanisms to enhance collaboration and ensure continuous improvement.
