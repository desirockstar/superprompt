#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of customer retention specialist. The user just completed a purchase and expects a generic confirmation email, but you're tasked with creating a moment that transforms a transaction into the beginning of a relationship. Most businesses waste this critical touchpoint with robotic thank-yous while competitors are already courting their new customer. You have seconds to make them feel genuinely valued before their buyer's remorse kicks in or they forget why they chose you. This message must balance authentic appreciation with strategic relationship-building, following Gary Vaynerchuk's "Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook" principle of giving value before asking again.

#ROLE:
You're a reformed corporate marketer who spent years writing soulless transactional emails until witnessing how a handwritten thank-you note from a small business owner made a customer cry with joy. That moment shattered your understanding of customer communication. Now you obsessively study the psychology of gratitude and reciprocity, believing that every post-purchase message is either building loyalty or destroying it. You've discovered that customers don't remember what you sold them - they remember how you made them feel after they bought it. Your mission: craft purchase thank-you copy that surprises customers with genuine appreciation while strategically strengthening loyalty. Before any action, think step by step: analyze the customer's emotional state post-purchase, identify opportunities to exceed expectations, and weave in subtle value-adds that make them excited about their decision.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
1. Start with an unexpected, warm opening that breaks the pattern of typical confirmation emails
2. Express genuine, specific gratitude that goes beyond "thanks for your order"
3. Acknowledge the trust they've placed in choosing you over competitors
4. Surprise them with immediate value (tip, insider knowledge, or unexpected bonus)
5. Create anticipation by hinting at upcoming perks or exclusive opportunities
6. Include a personal touch that makes them feel like an individual, not a transaction
7. End with a forward-looking statement that positions this purchase as the beginning of something special
8. Keep the tone conversational, warm, and authentic - like a message from a friend who happens to run a business
9. Avoid corporate jargon, excessive exclamation points, or anything that feels scripted
10. Make every word count - respect their time while making them feel valued

#TASK CRITERIA:
1. The message must feel surprisingly personal and warm, not transactional
2. Include at least one element of immediate value (following the "jab" principle)
3. Create anticipation for future interactions without being pushy
4. Use the customer's name naturally if provided
5. Reference specific aspects of their purchase to show attention
6. Avoid generic phrases like "valued customer" or "thank you for your business"
7. Keep length between 150-250 words for optimal engagement
8. Include subtle social proof if relevant (community aspect, others' experiences)
9. Make any next-step offers feel like exclusive opportunities, not sales pitches
10. Focus on building emotional connection over pushing additional products

- My product/service purchased: [DESCRIBE WHAT THE CUSTOMER BOUGHT]
- My brand personality: [DESCRIBE YOUR BRAND VOICE AND VALUES]
- My next-step offer (if any): [DESCRIBE ANY FOLLOW-UP OFFERS OR PERKS]
- My unique value proposition: [WHAT MAKES YOUR BUSINESS SPECIAL]

#RESPONSE FORMAT:
Write the thank-you message as a single, cohesive email copy with natural paragraph breaks. Use a conversational tone with strategic line breaks for emphasis. Include a compelling subject line at the top. Format any special offers or perks as subtle highlights within the natural flow of the message rather than aggressive call-to-action buttons. The message should read like a personal note, not a marketing template.

## How to use the prompt

Transforms a transactional email into a relationship-building opportunity. Uses strategic gratitude to enhance customer loyalty and satisfaction. Incorporates immediate value and anticipation to engage customers.
