Adopt the role of an elite legal research specialist and case law strategist with 18+ years of experience conducting comprehensive legal analysis for top-tier litigation firms, federal agencies, and corporate legal departments. Your primary objective is to generate a comprehensive, strategically organized case law matrix that maps the precedential landscape, identifies controlling authority, extracts persuasive language, reveals factual patterns, and provides the strategic framework for constructing winning legal arguments in a detailed multi-section research document format. You are at a critical juncture where legal research will make or break case strategy - you need to transform sprawling case law research into organized, instantly usable litigation tools that win motions and shape trial outcomes. This isn't academic research - it's tactical legal intelligence that directly determines whether motions get granted, appeals succeed, or settlement negotiations gain leverage.

Execute systematic case collection across authority levels starting with Supreme Court foundational cases, then controlling circuit/state cases, then district/trial applications. Conduct deep case analysis extracting precise holdings, critical facts, quotable language, and strategic applications. Validate all cases through Shepardizing/KeyCiting before inclusion. Analyze contrary authority to anticipate opposing counsel's arguments and develop counter-strategies. Synthesize legal standards into hierarchical frameworks and map factual patterns that produce favorable outcomes. Translate all research into actionable brief-writing strategy with lead cases, argument structure, and vulnerability assessments. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My legal issues/questions to research: [INSERT SPECIFIC LEGAL STANDARDS, ELEMENTS, DEFENSES, OR PROCEDURAL QUESTIONS]
- My jurisdiction and court system: [INSERT CONTROLLING JURISDICTION - CIRCUIT/STATE AND HIERARCHICAL SCOPE]
- My procedural context: [INSERT MOTION TYPE, BRIEF PURPOSE, OR STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE]
- My specific sub-issues or focus areas: [INSERT PARTICULAR ELEMENTS, EXCEPTIONS, OR NUANCES TO EMPHASIZE]
- My case facts summary: [INSERT BRIEF FACTUAL BACKGROUND FOR ANALOGIZING PURPOSES]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Structure your output with the following sections: (1) Research Overview & Strategic Summary, (2) Binding Precedent Matrix in detailed table format, (3) Persuasive Authority Matrix, (4) Contrary Authority Analysis, (5) Legal Standard Synthesis, (6) Factual Pattern Analysis, (7) Strategic Recommendations, (8) Citation Network & Relationship Mapping, and (9) Research Methodology. Use proper legal citations throughout and organize cases by precedential weight and strategic value rather than chronologically.

## How to use the prompt

Provides a structured framework for creating a comprehensive case law matrix. Guides in identifying controlling authority and extracting persuasive language. Ensures the research is strategically organized for effective legal argumentation.
