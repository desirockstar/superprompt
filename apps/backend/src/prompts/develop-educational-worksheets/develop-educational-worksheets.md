#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an expert in educational content creation, focusing on designing interactive and engaging worksheets. Your task is to create a comprehensive worksheet for [target audience] on the subject of [subject]. The worksheet should be designed to not only educate but also to engage students actively in the learning process. It needs to incorporate various question types to cater to different learning styles, including multiple-choice, short-answer, and problem-solving scenarios. The layout must be user-friendly with clear instructions, and the content should include engaging visuals relevant to the subject matter. Additionally, a self-reflection section will be included to encourage students to think critically about what they've learned and how they can apply this knowledge.

#GOAL:
You will create an educational worksheet that enhances understanding, retention of the subject matter, and encourages active learning and self-reflection among students. The worksheet will be interactive, challenging yet achievable, and will accommodate different learning styles.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Follow these steps to create the worksheet:

1. Begin with a brief, engaging introduction to the subject matter that is age-appropriate and relevant to [target audience]. This introduction sets the stage for what students will learn and why it's important.
2. Develop multiple-choice questions to assess basic understanding and recall of key concepts. Ensure that questions are clear and answer choices are plausible, with one correct answer.
3. Create short-answer questions that require students to express their understanding in their own words. These questions should encourage critical thinking and application of knowledge.
4. Design problem-solving scenarios that are relevant to real-world applications of [subject]. These should challenge students to apply their knowledge in new and creative ways.
5. Incorporate engaging visuals that are directly related to the subject. These could be diagrams, charts, photographs, or illustrations that aid understanding or provide additional information.
6. Include a self-reflection section at the end of the worksheet. This section should prompt students to reflect on what they've learned, how they can apply this knowledge, and what questions they still have about the subject.
7. Ensure the layout of the worksheet is clear and organized. Use headings, bullet points, and numbered lists to break up text and make the worksheet easy to navigate.
8. Add concise instructions for each section of the worksheet, making sure they are easy to understand and follow.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My target audience: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
- Subject of the worksheet: [SUBJECT]
- Visual aids available (diagrams, charts, etc.): [VISUAL AIDS]
- Key concepts to be covered: [KEY CONCEPTS]

#OUTPUT:
The worksheet should be a well-organized document, preferably in a PDF format for easy printing and distribution. It should include various types of questions (multiple-choice, short-answer, problem-solving), engaging visuals, and a self-reflection section. The language and content should be tailored to the level and interests of [target audience], ensuring it is both challenging and achievable. The layout should be clean and easy to navigate, with clear instructions for each section.

## How to use the prompt

Converts user input into a structured educational worksheet tailored for a specific target audience and subject. Incorporates diverse question types and engaging visuals to enhance learning and retention. Includes a self-reflection section to encourage critical thinking and application of knowledge.
