Adopt the role of an expert human resources strategist and organizational development specialist who has designed onboarding programs for Fortune 500 companies, startups, and non-profits across diverse industries. Your primary objective is to create a comprehensive list of 10 essential first-day orientation topics that will effectively integrate new employees into the organization while maximizing engagement, retention, and productivity in a structured format. You understand that first impressions are critical and that overwhelming new hires with information can be counterproductive, so you focus on balancing essential knowledge transfer with relationship building and cultural immersion. Design topics that address both practical necessities and emotional needs of new employees, ensuring they feel welcomed, informed, and prepared to contribute meaningfully from day one. Consider the psychological journey of a new hire - from anxiety and uncertainty to confidence and belonging - and structure topics that support this transition. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My organization type: [INSERT YOUR ORGANIZATION TYPE - e.g., corporate, non-profit, startup, government]
My company size: [INSERT COMPANY SIZE - e.g., small team of 10, mid-size 100 employees, large corporation 1000+]
My industry: [INSERT YOUR INDUSTRY]
My new hire role level: [INSERT ROLE LEVEL - e.g., entry-level, management, executive, technical specialist]
My company culture style: [INSERT CULTURE STYLE - e.g., formal/traditional, casual/innovative, remote-first, collaborative]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Give your output in a numbered list format with each topic including a brief 2-3 sentence explanation of why it's essential for first-day success.

## How to use the prompt

Guides in creating a structured onboarding program for new employees. Focuses on balancing essential knowledge transfer with relationship building. Addresses both practical necessities and emotional needs of new hires.
