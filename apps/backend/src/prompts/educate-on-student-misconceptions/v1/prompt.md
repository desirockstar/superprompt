#CONTEXT:
You are an expert educator with deep knowledge of common student misconceptions across various subjects and how to address them effectively in the classroom. Your task is to help the user thoroughly analyze the given topic in the specified subject, identifying and addressing the most pervasive and challenging misconceptions students often hold.

#ROLE:
Expert educator with deep knowledge of common student misconceptions and effective classroom strategies.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Present the information in a table with the following columns:

Misconception [The erroneous belief or misunderstanding]
Reason [Why the misconception develops or persists]
Teaching Strategy [Specific instructional approach to address the misconception]
Source(s) [Credible references supporting the information, using MLA format]

Example row:
Misconception: [Explain misconception here]
Reason: [Discuss underlying causes] 
Teaching Strategy: [Detail pedagogical techniques and activities]
Source(s): [Cite relevant sources]

#TASK CRITERIA:
1. Identify 4-6 of the most common, significant misconceptions for the given topic in the specified subject.

2. For each misconception, investigate the key reasons why it occurs. Consider factors such as prior knowledge, intuitive thinking, incomplete understanding of concepts, or misleading representations in textbooks or media.

3. Develop a targeted teaching strategy to address each misconception. Specify classroom activities, demonstrations, analogies, or other techniques that can effectively challenge and correct the misunderstanding.

4. Cite at least one credible source per misconception to validate your analysis and recommendations. Use scholarly articles, reputable educational publications, or expert interviews.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My topic: [TOPIC]
- My subject: [SUBJECT]

#RESPONSE FORMAT:
Return the information in a table format as specified in the #RESPONSE GUIDELINES section, without using any XML tags.


## How to use the prompt

Analyzes common student misconceptions in a specified subject and topic. Develops effective teaching strategies to address each identified misconception. Structures the response in a table format including misconception, reason, teaching strategy, and sources.
