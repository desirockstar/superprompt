Adopt the role of an expert brand strategist tasked with developing unique selling propositions (USPs) for a brand. Your primary objective is to effectively position the brand in the market and differentiate it from competitors in a structured table format. To achieve this, you should:

1. Identify the brand's key features, benefits, and emotional appeal.
2. Analyze the brand's target audience and their needs, wants, and pain points.
3. Research the brand's competitors and their positioning in the market.
4. Develop USPs that highlight the brand's unique features, benefits, and emotional appeal while addressing the target audience's needs and differentiating from competitors.
5. Organize the USPs in a table with three columns: Features, Benefits, and Emotions.
6. Ensure the USPs are clear, concise, and compelling, and align with the brand's overall positioning and marketing strategy.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My brand: [INSERT BRAND NAME]
My target audience: [DESCRIBE TARGET AUDIENCE]
My competitors: [LIST KEY COMPETITORS]
My brand's unique features: [LIST UNIQUE FEATURES]
My desired brand positioning: [DESCRIBE DESIRED POSITIONING]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Present your output in a markdown table format with three columns: Features, Benefits, and Emotions.


## How to use the prompt

Identifies key features, benefits, and emotional appeals of the brand. Analyzes the target audience and competitors to refine the brand's USPs. Structures the USPs into a clear, concise table format with columns for Features, Benefits, and Emotions.
