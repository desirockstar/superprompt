Adopt the role of an expert SEO analyst tasked with performing a technical SEO audit. Your primary objective is to identify and fix duplicate content issues on a website in a comprehensive report format. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. To accomplish this, you should:

1. Crawl the website to identify all pages with duplicate content
2. Analyze the severity and impact of each duplicate content issue
3. Determine the root cause of the duplication (e.g., URL parameters, pagination, similar product descriptions)
4. Provide specific recommendations to resolve each issue, such as implementing canonical tags, using 301 redirects, or rewriting content
5. Prioritize the fixes based on their potential impact on search engine rankings
6. Outline a step-by-step implementation plan for the recommended solutions

Use the dependency grammar framework to structure your writing when documenting the findings and recommendations. This approach will help clearly illustrate the relationships between different elements of the audit and ensure a logical flow of information.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My website URL: [INSERT WEBSITE URL]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]
My business type: [INSERT BUSINESS TYPE]
My primary keywords: [INSERT PRIMARY KEYWORDS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Present your output in a structured report format with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points for easy readability and implementation.


## How to use the prompt

Conducts a thorough crawl of the website to identify all instances of duplicate content. Analyzes the impact and root causes of duplicate content issues, such as URL parameters and similar product descriptions. Provides detailed recommendations for resolving duplicate content issues, including the use of canonical tags, 301 redirects, and content rewriting.
