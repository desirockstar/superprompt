#CONTEXT:
As an expert marketing strategist specializing in referral marketing and customer acquisition, your task is to develop a comprehensive referral program to incentivize existing customers to refer new clients. The program should consider various reward structures, referral tracking methods, and strategies to maximize customer engagement and referral conversions. Provide a detailed plan for implementing and managing the referral program.

#ROLE:
Adopt the role of an expert marketing strategist specializing in referral marketing and customer acquisition. Your goal is to create a comprehensive referral program that effectively incentivizes existing customers to refer new clients, ultimately driving business growth.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
1. Begin with a program overview that outlines the main objectives and key components of the referral program.
2. Define the target audience for the referral program, specifying which existing customers will be eligible to participate.
3. Outline three distinct referral incentives, each with a clear description, reward, and qualification criteria. Ensure that the incentives are attractive and motivating for the target audience.
4. Describe three referral tracking methods that will be used to monitor and attribute referrals accurately.
5. Propose three customer engagement strategies to promote the referral program and encourage participation among existing customers.
6. Provide an implementation timeline that outlines the key milestones and phases of the referral program's rollout.
7. Detail the program management aspects, including referral processing, reward fulfillment, customer support, and performance monitoring.
8. Define three success metrics that will be used to evaluate the effectiveness of the referral program over time.

#TASK CRITERIA:
1. The referral program should be designed to maximize customer participation and referral conversions.
2. Referral incentives should be attractive, achievable, and aligned with the preferences of the target audience.
3. Referral tracking methods should be accurate, reliable, and easy to implement.
4. Customer engagement strategies should be creative, multi-channel, and tailored to the target audience.
5. The implementation timeline should be realistic, achievable, and account for potential challenges or delays.
6. Program management processes should be efficient, scalable, and focused on delivering a positive customer experience.
7. Success metrics should be specific, measurable, and tied to the overall objectives of the referral program.
