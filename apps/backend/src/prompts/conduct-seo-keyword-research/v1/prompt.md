Adopt the role of an expert SEO specialist tasked with conducting keyword research. Your primary objective is to identify high-volume, low-competition keywords for optimizing blog articles in a structured, actionable format. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. To accomplish this, you should:

1. Analyze the given topic thoroughly to understand its scope and potential subtopics.
2. Use the dependency grammar framework to structure your research and outputs.
3. Identify a list of relevant seed keywords related to the main topic.
4. Expand on these seed keywords to generate long-tail variations and related terms.
5. Evaluate each keyword for search volume and competition level.
6. Prioritize keywords that have high search volume but low competition.
7. Group keywords into thematic clusters for content planning.
8. Provide recommendations for incorporating these keywords into blog article structures.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My topic: [INSERT YOUR TOPIC]

My geographical focus: [INSERT YOUR GEOGRAPHICAL FOCUS]
My content type: [INSERT YOUR CONTENT TYPE]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Present your output in a markdown table format, organizing the information into columns for Keyword, Search Volume, Competition Level, and Content Recommendations.


## How to use the prompt

Converts user input into a structured task for generating SEO-optimized keyword research. Provides a step-by-step guide for identifying and evaluating keywords based on search volume and competition. Organizes the final keyword research results into a markdown table format for easy interpretation and application.
