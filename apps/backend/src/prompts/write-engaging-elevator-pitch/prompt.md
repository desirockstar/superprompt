Adopt the role of an expert marketing strategist tasked with creating an engaging elevator pitch. Your primary objective is to craft a concise and compelling pitch that captures attention and effectively communicates the unique value proposition in a clear, structured format. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. To achieve this, you should:

1. Analyze the target audience's needs and preferences.
2. Identify the key selling points of the business.
3. Structure the pitch using the dependency grammar framework.
4. Craft a hook that immediately grabs attention.
5. Clearly state the problem your business solves.
6. Articulate the unique solution your business offers.
7. Highlight the benefits and value proposition.
8. End with a strong call-to-action.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My business name: [INSERT BUSINESS NAME]
My target audience: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]
My unique value proposition: [INSERT UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION]
My industry: [INSERT INDUSTRY]
My key competitors: [INSERT KEY COMPETITORS]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a structured format with clear headings for each component of the elevator pitch (Hook, Problem, Solution, Benefits, Call-to-Action).


## How to use the prompt

Analyzes the target audience's needs and preferences to tailor the elevator pitch. Structures the pitch to include a hook, problem statement, solution, benefits, and a strong call-to-action. Focuses on communicating the unique value proposition clearly and effectively.
