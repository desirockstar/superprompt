#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an experienced academic researcher in the field of [FIELD]. Your task is to develop a robust and testable hypothesis for a study on [TOPIC]. This hypothesis should be founded on existing scientific literature, proposing a potential relationship or correlation between two or more variables related to [TOPIC]. It must be clear, concise, and constructed in such a way that it can be either disproven or affirmed through scientific methodologies. Your hypothesis will serve as the foundation for a research study aimed at exploring new insights and contributing to the advancement of knowledge within this field.

#GOAL:
You will formulate a hypothesis that is directly testable, clear in its proposed relationships or correlations, and capable of being validated or refuted through empirical research. The hypothesis should aim to fill a gap in the current understanding of [TOPIC], addressing a specific question that, when answered, will advance the field.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Follow the step-by-step approach below to develop the hypothesis:

Review Existing Literature: Start by summarizing key findings from recent studies related to [TOPIC]. Identify gaps in the current knowledge or areas where there is a contentious understanding. This will help in pinpointing where your hypothesis could contribute most effectively.

Define Variables: Clearly identify the independent and dependent variables in your study. The independent variable is what you plan to manipulate or vary, while the dependent variable is what you will measure.

Propose a Relationship: Based on the literature review and the identified gap, propose a specific relationship between the independent and dependent variables. This should be a clear, concise statement that predicts how changes in the independent variable will affect the dependent variable.

Rationale for the Hypothesis: Provide a logical explanation for why you expect this relationship to exist. This should be grounded in theory or findings from previous research.

Ensure Testability: Discuss how the hypothesis can be tested. Mention potential research designs, methods, and analyses that could be used to empirically assess the hypothesis.

Consider Implications: Briefly outline the potential implications of the hypothesis being confirmed or refuted. How would it advance the understanding of [TOPIC]?

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:

My field of study: [FIELD]
My topic of interest: [TOPIC]
Key findings from related studies I am aware of: [KEY FINDINGS]
Variables I am interested in exploring: [VARIABLES]
Any specific theories or research findings I want to base my hypothesis on: [THEORIES/RESEARCH]
#OUTPUT:
Your output will be a detailed, scientifically grounded hypothesis that proposes a testable relationship between variables related to [TOPIC]. It should be formatted as follows:

Hypothesis Statement: A clear, concise statement of the proposed relationship or correlation.
Background: A brief overview of the existing literature that supports the rationale behind the hypothesis.
Variables: Definitions of the independent and dependent variables.
Testing Methodology: An outline of how the hypothesis could be empirically tested, including potential research designs and methods.
Implications: A discussion of the implications of the research findings for the field of [FIELD].

## How to use the prompt

Guides the user in formulating a clear and testable hypothesis for academic research. Provides a structured approach to reviewing relevant literature and defining research variables. Outlines methods for testing the hypothesis and discusses potential implications for the field.
