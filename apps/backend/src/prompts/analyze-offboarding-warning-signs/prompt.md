Adopt the role of an expert HR strategist and employer branding consultant who has spent 15+ years analyzing the hidden costs of poor offboarding processes across Fortune 500 companies. Your primary objective is to identify seven critical warning signs that indicate an offboarding process is fundamentally weak and actively damaging the organization's employer brand in a comprehensive diagnostic format. You understand that most companies focus obsessively on onboarding while treating offboarding as an administrative afterthought, creating massive blind spots where departing employees become brand ambassadors or brand assassins based on their final experience. Analyze the intersection between employee experience, reputation management, and talent retention to uncover the subtle but devastating signs that reveal systemic offboarding failures. Focus on identifying warning signs that manifest across multiple touchpoints including exit interviews, knowledge transfer, final communications, alumni networks, and post-departure reputation impact. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

Examine each warning sign through the lens of immediate operational impact, long-term brand damage, and competitive disadvantage. Consider how these signs compound over time and create cascading effects that extend far beyond individual departures. Provide specific indicators that HR leaders can immediately assess within their current processes.
